This blog is neither about construction nor architecture. 

As a software engineer and a postgraduate research student at the [University of Strathclyde](https://www.strath.ac.uk/) in Glasgow I created this site primarly to develop my writing skills by sharing solutions to technical challenges solved by me at work. Hopefully reading this blog will help you to learn someting new or at least save time if you are about to face similar problems.

You can meet me in the [Intelligent Computational Engineering Laboratory](http://icelab.uk/) at the 8th floor of the [James Weir Building](https://www.google.co.uk/maps/place/James+Weir+Building,+75+Montrose+St,+Glasgow+G1+1XQ), where I work on the [Robust Data-Driven Combinatorial Optimization](http://icelab.uk/projects/research-projects/robust-dddco/) project. Before moving to Scotland I used to work in industry. For more information on my previous projects and technical expertise please visit my [LinkedIn profile](https://www.linkedin.com/in/mateusz-polnik-a43067a4).

The title of the blog was inspired by the following quotation:

> Like the elite of ancient Egypt, most people in most cultures dedicated their lives to building pyramids. Only the names, shapes and sizes of these pyramids change from one culture to the other.
> --- Yuval Noah Harari, [Sapiens. A Brief History of Humankind](http://www.goodreads.com/book/show/23692271-sapiens)

If you indeed look for guidelies how to build a pyramid I refer you to the excellent state of the art survey of the Egyptian pyramid construction techniques in the [Wikipedia article](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Egyptian_pyramid_construction_techniques).
